## Book Outline:  Swipe Right, Wrong Guy? A Millennial Rom-Com

**Target Audience:** Women aged 25-40, familiar with dating apps, remote work culture, and millennial anxieties.

**Reading Level:**  High school/early college.  Tone:  Witty, relatable, slightly cynical but ultimately hopeful.

**Core Themes:**  Finding love in unexpected places, embracing vulnerability, navigating the complexities of modern relationships, self-acceptance, the illusion vs. reality of online personas.


**Book Title & Subtitle Suggestions:**

* Swipe Right, Wrong Guy?  A Millennial Love Story
* Algorithmic Attraction: Finding Love in the Digital Age
* From Swipe to Soulmate: A Modern Romance
* The Glitch in the System: A Millennial Love Story


**Character Profiles:**

* **Ava Sharma:**  32, successful freelance graphic designer, cynical but secretly romantic, struggling to balance her career with her dating life.  Highly organized in her work, chaotic in her personal life.  Uses dating apps strategically but is often disappointed.  Secretly yearns for a genuine connection.

* **Liam O'Connell:** 35, charmingly awkward software developer, works remotely, socially awkward but incredibly kind and intelligent.  Avoids commitment, prefers virtual interactions to real-life ones.  Has a hidden artistic side he keeps secret.  Underlying insecurity masks a deep desire for connection.


**Chapter Breakdown:**

**Part 1: The Swipe Heard 'Round the World**

1. **The Algorithm's Cruel Joke:** Ava, frustrated with dating apps, matches with Liam – a profile that's both intriguing and utterly perplexing.  Their initial interactions are filled with witty banter and mutual misunderstandings.

2. **Virtual Chemistry, Real-Life Anxiety:** Ava and Liam's online conversations blossom, but the prospect of a real-life date fills Ava with dread. Liam struggles to translate his online charm into real-world interaction.

3. **The First (Awkward) Date:** Their first date is a comedy of errors, highlighting their contrasting personalities and communication styles. Despite the initial hiccups, a spark ignites.

4. **The Ghost in the Machine:**  Misunderstandings and miscommunications lead to a brief period of ghosting, forcing both to confront their insecurities and communication styles.

5. **Unexpected Connections:**  A shared passion for obscure 80s movies unexpectedly brings them closer, revealing unexpected common ground beyond their digital personas.


**Part 2:  From Pixels to Personalities**

6. **Remote Romance:** Their relationship develops primarily through virtual interactions – video calls, online games, shared playlists – challenging traditional notions of dating.

7. **The Friend Zone Labyrinth:** Ava's best friend, Maya, provides hilarious yet insightful commentary on Ava's relationship, adding comedic relief and external perspective.

8. **The Ex-Factor:** Liam's past relationship resurfaces, creating tension and forcing him to confront unresolved emotional baggage.

9. **Real-World Challenges:**  They navigate the complexities of balancing remote work, personal lives, and a burgeoning relationship, highlighting the difficulties of modern life.

10. **The Great Misunderstanding:** A significant miscommunication threatens to derail their relationship, forcing them to confront their communication issues head-on.


**Part 3:  Love in the Digital Age**

11. **Unmasking the Persona:**  Ava and Liam learn to accept each other's flaws and vulnerabilities, moving beyond the idealized versions presented online.

12. **The Grand Gesture (Sort Of):** Liam attempts a romantic gesture that hilariously backfires, but ultimately reveals his genuine feelings.

13. **Facing the Future:** They confront their fears about commitment and the future, acknowledging the challenges and rewards of a long-term relationship.

14. **The Unexpected Proposal (Maybe):** A surprising turn of events leads to an unexpected and unconventional proposal.

15. **Happily Ever After (Or At Least, A Really Good Start):** The novel concludes with a hopeful and satisfying resolution, emphasizing the importance of genuine connection over superficial appearances.


This outline provides a framework for a romantic comedy novel targeting the specified audience.  The word count per chapter will need to be adjusted to achieve the desired overall length (approximately 30,000 words).  The specific events and dialogue will be fleshed out during the writing phase.
